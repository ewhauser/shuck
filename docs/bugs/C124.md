## Delta Analysis

### Dead-code spans are over-reported across whole branches `[shuck-fix]`

**Bucket:** Shuck-only  
**Impact:** 787 locations / 68 scripts in the sample

The sampled `C124` failures are almost entirely shuck-only unreachable-code spans. ShellCheck stays silent, while shuck reports large stretches of code after exits, returns, or other control-flow endings. The common pattern is that the diagnostic spans run much wider than the specific unreachable statement, often covering whole branches or follow-on blocks.

The rule implementation is very small:
`unreachable_after_exit.rs` just forwards whatever `semantic.dead_code()` returns. That strongly suggests the bug is upstream in the semantic dead-code computation rather than in the linter wrapper itself.

Representative examples:
- `HariSekhon__DevOps-Bash-tools__setup__ci_bootstrap.sh:41` - a short post-exit region is reported even though ShellCheck stays quiet.
- `SlackBuildsOrg__slackbuilds__development__quickjs__quickjs.SlackBuild:82` - one script produces three separate unreachable-code spans, including a whole trailing branch.
- `SlackBuildsOrg__slackbuilds__network__pCloudDrive__pCloudDrive.SlackBuild:48` - many consecutive regions are marked unreachable, suggesting the dead-code pass is walking too far past the actual exit point.
- `acmesh-official__acme.sh__notify__smtp.sh:111` - large chunks of the notification function are treated as unreachable.
- `scop__bash-completion__completions-fallback__slackpkg.bash:36` - a completion helper file produces a long tail of unreachable spans.

### Helper-file and shell-collapse cases are a separate environment tail `[environment]`

**Bucket:** Environment  
**Impact:** 11 parse-abort scripts, plus helper-heavy scripts with `project-closure`, `directive-handling`, and `unknown-shell-collapse` labels

The sample also includes parser failures on malformed or non-shell inputs, plus helper-file style scripts where the corpus labels indicate project-closure or shell-detection ambiguity. Those are useful to keep visible, but they are not enough on their own to explain the main `C124` gap.

Representative examples:
- `GameServerManagers__LinuxGSM__lgsm__modules__alert_ifttt.sh` - unterminated command substitution.
- `HariSekhon__DevOps-Bash-tools__git__git_diff_commit.sh` - unterminated double quote.
- `SlackBuildsOrg__slackbuilds__games__njam__patches__drop_gda.patch` - parser expected a word.
- `scop__bash-completion__completions-fallback__slackpkg.bash` - labeled `project-closure` and `unknown-shell-collapse`.
- `void-linux__void-packages__common__hooks__post-install__01-remove-misc.sh` - labeled `unknown-shell-collapse`.
